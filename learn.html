🎵 The Staff & Note Names

What is a staff?

Music is written on a staff — 5 lines and 4 spaces. Notes sit on the lines or in the spaces. The higher the note on the staff, the higher it sounds.

Treble Clef (𝄞) — right hand / higher notes

The treble clef curls around the G line (second from bottom) — that's why it's also called the G clef.

🧠 Memory Trick — Lines (bottom → top)
Every Good Boy Deserves Fruit
→ E · G · B · D · F
🧠 Memory Trick — Spaces (bottom → top)
The spaces spell a word: F · A · C · E = FACE 😄

Bass Clef (𝄢) — left hand / lower notes

The bass clef's two dots surround the F line (fourth from bottom) — also called the F clef.

🧠 Memory Trick — Lines (bottom → top)
Good Boys Deserve Fine Apples
→ G · B · D · F · A
🧠 Memory Trick — Spaces (bottom → top)
All Cows Eat Grass → A · C · E · G

Middle C

Middle C sits on a short ledger line below the treble clef or above the bass clef. It's the C in the middle of the piano — a great landmark note!

🧠 Trick
Count up from the bottom of the treble clef: the first ledger line below the staff is Middle C (C4). Count down from the top of the bass clef: the first ledger line above is also Middle C.

🧠 Trick — halving
Each shorter note is half the value of the one above: Semibreve → Minim → Crotchet → Quaver → Semiquaver. Double a note to go the other way!

Dotted notes

A dot after a note adds half of that note's value to it.

Note= Note + …Total beats
Dotted minim ♩.Minim + crotchet3 beats
Dotted crotchet ♩.Crotchet + quaver1½ beats
Dotted quaver ♩.Quaver + semiquaver¾ beat
🧠 Trick
Dot = add half. If the note is worth 2, the dot adds 1. If it's worth 1, the dot adds ½.

Rests — silence

Every note value has a matching rest (silence). The semibreve rest hangs from the 4th line; the minim rest sits on the 3rd line.

🧠 Trick — semibreve vs minim rest
The semibreve rest hangs DOWN like a heavy brick (it's heavier = 4 beats).
The minim rest sits UP like a hat (lighter = 2 beats).
"The heavy one hangs, the light one sits."
⏱ Time Signatures

The time signature appears at the start of a piece. It tells you:

🔝 Top number = how many beats per bar
🔽 Bottom number = what note gets one beat (4 = crotchet, 8 = quaver, 2 = minim)

🧠 Trick
The bottom number is like a fraction: 4 means "quarter" (crotchet), 8 means "eighth" (quaver), 2 means "half" (minim).

🧠 Trick — sharps keys
The key name is one semitone above the last sharp. E.g. the last sharp in D major is C# — one semitone above C# is D. ✓
🧠 Trick — flats keys
The key name is the second-to-last flat. E.g. Bb major has 2 flats (Bb Eb) — the second-to-last is Bb. ✓ (Exception: F major = 1 flat, just memorise it!)

Scale patterns

Every major scale follows the same pattern of Tones (T = whole step) and Semitones (S = half step):

TTS TTTS
🧠 Trick
Two tones, semitone, three tones, semitone. The semitones are always between steps 3–4 and 7–8.

Harmonic minor scale Grade 2

TST TST+SS

The harmonic minor raises the 7th degree by a semitone, creating an augmented 2nd (3 semitones) between degrees 6 and 7. This gives it an exotic, dramatic sound!

📐 Intervals

How to count an interval

Count every letter name from the bottom note to the top note, including both notes.

Example: C → G = C(1) D(2) E(3) F(4) G(5) = a 5th.

🧠 Trick
Always count the bottom note as "1". Never start at 0!

🎹 Chords & Cadences

Building a triad

A triad is a 3-note chord built by stacking 3rds: root, 3rd, 5th.

Type3rd above root5th above rootSound
MajorMajor 3rd (4 semitones)Perfect 5th (7 semitones)Happy, bright
MinorMinor 3rd (3 semitones)Perfect 5th (7 semitones)Sad, dark
🧠 Trick
Major chord = big 3rd + small 3rd stacked (4+3 semitones).
Minor chord = small 3rd + big 3rd stacked (3+4 semitones). Just swap the order!

🎵 Cadences

What is a cadence?

A cadence is a pair of chords that ends a phrase — like a musical full stop or comma. The chord built on each degree of the scale has a Roman numeral:

DegreeRoman numeralIn C major
1st (tonic)IC major
4th (subdominant)IVF major
5th (dominant)VG major
6th (submediant)VIA minor

Perfect Cadence — V → I G1

The perfect cadence (also called an authentic cadence) goes from chord V to chord I. It sounds finished — like a musical full stop.

🧠 Memory Trick
V → I = "done!" — think of it as coming home. In C major: G major → C major. It's the most common ending in music.
🎵 Sound like…
The ending of "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star" — that final G → C is a perfect cadence!

Plagal Cadence — IV → I G1

The plagal cadence goes from chord IV to chord I. It sounds gentle and settled — softer than a perfect cadence.

🧠 Memory Trick
IV → I = "Amen" — this is the "Amen" cadence you hear at the end of hymns. In C major: F major → C major.

Imperfect Cadence — ends on V G3

An imperfect cadence ends on chord V. It sounds unfinished — like a musical comma, leaving you expecting more.

🧠 Memory Trick
Ends on V = "not finished yet…" Any chord → V. Common: I → V or IV → V. In C major: C major → G major.

Interrupted Cadence — V → VI G3

The interrupted cadence (also called a deceptive cadence) goes from chord V to chord VI — a surprise! You expect V → I, but get VI instead.

🧠 Memory Trick
V → VI = "surprise!" — the music tricks your ear. In C major: G major → A minor. Listen for the "wait, that's not right!" feeling.

Reading chord diagrams

Chord diagrams show the guitar neck from the front:

  • Vertical lines = strings (left = low E, right = high e)
  • Horizontal lines = frets
  • ● dots = where to press your finger
  • O above a string = play it open (unfretted)
  • ✕ above a string = don't play this string
  • A number above the dots = which finger to use (1=index, 2=middle, 3=ring, 4=pinky)

How chords relate to piano theory

The chord names are the same! A G major chord on guitar has the same notes (G, B, D) as on piano — just played differently. All the music theory you learn for piano applies to guitar too.

🧠 Trick — major vs minor guitar chords
On guitar, chords with a lowercase "m" are minor (e.g. Am, Em, Dm). No "m" = major (G, C, D, A, E). It's exactly the same distinction as on piano!
